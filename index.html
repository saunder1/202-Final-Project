Scroll on to explore the rise and fall of one of the most unique and beloved video game titles in the past decade.

Overwatch launches with 21 unique heroes you can play as, now with 37.

The game releases in May 2016 and wins Game of the Year in December.

The Overwatch League's inaugral season is in January 2018, the first pro-gaming league of its kind.

Blizzcon 2019: Game Director Jeff Kaplan announces Overwatch 2!

Overwatch 2 will have a singleplayer campaign (PvE) mode in addition to the competitive multiplayer.

The Campaign mode will have "endlessly replayable" hero-missions with hero skill trees allowing unique playthroughs.

There would be all new enemies to fight, new abilities, and new maps. Players that were not as interested in competitive PvP were very excited.

Then, blizzard goes quiet...
Although there are still many players, updates and new content for the game slows to a crawl...
supposedly, the devs are hard at work on Overwatch 2...
finally, in march 2022, the release date for Overwatch 2 is announced!

OCTOBER 2022:

OW2 Releases

Blizzard launches Overwatch 2 with only the competitive PvP, saying the PvE will come later.

Many call it Overwatch 1.5 because it's virtually the same game but with a balance update.

The multiplayer is now 5v5 instead of 6v6, there is 1 new gamemode, and some heroes were changed.

The players accept this and the new, increasingly standard, battle pass model because "PvE is coming."

Six months later...

Blizzard Announces that the PvE mode as they promised is not going to be delivered.

The fallout was swift. The gaming community buzzed with discussions. Players, including loyal content creators, threatened to quit. It felt like a betrayal. Blizzard made empty promises, disappointing everyone. Despite Overwatch 2's profitable model, they chose not to invest in the new mode. Corporate greed has once again hindered a great game's potential.
